Context

The UC¬†San Diego Library is among the top of the nation's public academic libraries, providing resources and services that support the research and education of the UCSD¬†community. To accommodate the needs of the growing community at UCSD, the Library administration created the Geisel Library Revitalization Initiative (GLRI) to set a plan of motions to transform the Library into an active and engaging hub for its users.

Part of the GLRI involves creating a physical service hub, in which users can find help at primary service points to help them with their needs in accessing services and resources from the Library. However, the virtual space (library.ucsd.edu) needs modifications to align the availability and efficiency of help service with that of the new physical hub.

My contribution

This was one of the first projects behind the success of my agency, Fi. With a team of 8 design students, I took the role as a project manager, while still actively participating in the UX process.

This project took 3 months to present research findings, and still is ongoing into the design process. I have learned beyond the UX process, in managing a team, working with clients and deadlines, and creating valuable insights for Library staff to learn from.

A lot of groundwork was done to align the agency team with the goals of the Digital UX Librarian, so to keep this short and sweet for you, attached is the report I outlined and put together using the team's research.

Future of the project

As we move to use the research findings in our application of the UX design process for finding a better method of displaying help on the site, we have also been gaining recognition and traction within the Library itself.

This turned out to be more than just an ordinary case study for the students involved. Rather, it has provided guidance for the Digital UX¬†Librarian to provide evidence to the Library administration that this is a gap that needs to be filled in to address the effectiveness of the GLRI.
